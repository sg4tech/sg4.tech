# Why nothing ships

## TL;DR

When nothing seems to ship, the issue is usually not effort. It is fragmented ownership, overloaded teams, and an execution system that rewards motion more than completed outcomes.

## Symptoms

- Lots of tickets move, but customers see little change.
- Teams report activity, while commercial stakeholders see delay.
- Work stalls in handoffs, approvals, and rework loops.

## Why this happens

Output falls when teams start too much, depend on too many cross-functional decisions, and lack a strong definition of done.

## What most companies do wrong

Leaders often respond by adding meetings, asking for more detailed reporting, or splitting work into even smaller pieces without resolving the underlying coordination problem.

## How to diagnose

Trace one initiative from idea to release and measure where it waits, loops, or loses ownership.

## What actually works

Tighter scope, fewer parallel bets, and clear accountability for customer-visible outcomes usually restore momentum faster than new process layers.
