# Why engineering is slow

## TL;DR

Your engineering team is often slower than the business expects because work enters the system faster than it leaves, ownership is unclear, and planning quality is too low for predictable execution.

## Symptoms

- Roadmap items take longer than anyone expected.
- Teams stay busy, but priorities keep shifting.
- Founders hear status updates without gaining confidence.

## Why this happens

Slow engineering is rarely caused by individual effort. It is more often a systems problem: too many active priorities, fuzzy ownership, and delivery plans built on assumptions that were never tested.

## What most companies do wrong

Many companies respond by pushing harder or adding process on top of confusion. That usually increases reporting without improving flow.

## How to diagnose

Look at how work is queued, who makes tradeoff decisions, and how often priorities change after implementation starts.

## What actually works

The useful move is to reduce simultaneous work, assign clear ownership, and make delivery tradeoffs visible in business terms.
