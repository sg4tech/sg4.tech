# Why hiring more engineers doesn't help

## TL;DR

Adding engineers to an unclear delivery system usually increases communication overhead faster than it increases throughput.

## Symptoms

- Headcount grows while delivery speed stays flat.
- New hires need long ramp-up periods to become effective.
- Founders expect acceleration but experience more coordination work.

## Why this happens

More people only help when priorities, interfaces, and ownership are already stable. Otherwise, each additional hire adds more dependency management and decision traffic.

## What most companies do wrong

Companies often treat hiring as the default answer to missed deadlines without first checking whether the bottleneck is decision-making, planning, or team structure.

## How to diagnose

Compare growth in team size with growth in shipped outcomes, and review whether the org can clearly explain who owns what.

## What actually works

Fixing the operating model first often creates more capacity than hiring, because it reduces waste before adding cost.
